# Deliverable — Copy Rewrite & Implementation Directives

Purpose: fix authenticity gaps in the current copy (personal stats presented as company history, unverifiable claims, "studio" framing that doesn't match the actual business model, an internal contradiction on retainers). Positioning shift: from "boutique production studio" to "production service" — reliable, repeatable video capacity a team can call on, not a one-off creative engagement.

No personal name or founder bio is used anywhere in this version, by request. Trust is carried by process specificity and consistent "we" voice instead — not a founder story.

---

## 1. GLOBAL DIRECTIVES — apply these first, across all pages

1. Find/replace `studio` → `service` everywhere in body copy and meta/SEO tags. Exception: the brand wordmark "Deliverable" and the nav logo stay as-is.
2. Delete the 7+ / 30+ / 50+ stat cards in their current form. Those numbers are founder career history, not Deliverable's track record, and the site presents them as the latter. Do not replace with a bio. Leave the stat section empty until there are real Deliverable-specific numbers (videos delivered, clients served, concept demos built).
3. Delete or rewrite any line implying AI avatar render quality is the differentiator (e.g. "we know where AI avatars go wrong"). Current-gen avatar tools (HeyGen v5+) already produce strong output by default — that's a commodity baseline now, not a service differentiator. Differentiation moves to: script/instructional design quality, process reliability, and the update-without-reshoot workflow.
4. Every video under "Our Work" must carry a visible label until real client work exists: **"Concept demo — built to show what's possible, not a client project."** Only swap to "Client project" once it actually is one.
5. Fix the retainer contradiction: the hero checklist offers "retainer or per project" pricing, but the contact section says "No long contracts or agency retainers" — those conflict. Since the positioning is now a service (not one-off studio gigs), retainers should be framed as available, not avoided. See Section 7 for the corrected line.
6. Check the line "Working with clients in Australia, UK, and Singapore" for accuracy. If these are target markets rather than current clients, change to "Open to working with clients in Australia, UK, and Singapore" — don't imply an existing client base that doesn't exist yet. Same authenticity standard as the stats issue above.
7. Build at least one concept demo per listed segment (HR, Training, Professional services, SME) with visibly different avatars/voices and at least one using B-roll or motion graphics layered in — not the same avatar reading four different scripts. Four identical-format demos undercuts the "production team, not a tool reseller" claim instead of proving it.

## 9. OPEN ITEMS — decisions for you, not copy fixes

1. **Pricing transparency.** Staying vague on price reduces self-qualification (visitors can't tell if they're in budget range before reaching out). Adding even a directional floor ("projects typically start around $X") cuts friction. Pick one.
2. **"First concept free" scope.** Unbounded free work doesn't scale for a solo operation. Decide whether to qualify leads first (briefing call before free concept), cap scope of the free concept, or leave uncapped and absorb the cost as acquisition spend.
3. **"Why Deliverable" section** wasn't visible in the screenshots shared, so it isn't rewritten here. Send its current copy if it needs the same pass — likely candidate for cutting if it duplicates the comparison table.
4. **Stat cards.** Once concept demos exist, an honest replacement is something like "4 concept videos across 4 industries" — true, countable, and doesn't borrow personal history.
5. **Segment order.** Confirm Training providers as the lead segment, or pick a different one — but pick one rather than presenting all four with equal weight.
