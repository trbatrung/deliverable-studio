# Deliverable — Project Brief & Content Handoff

## What This Is
Deliverable is a B2B AI video production brand. Target market: international SMEs in Australia, UK, and Singapore who need training videos, onboarding content, and course modules without hiring a production crew.

**Tagline:** "AI video solutions for businesses that move fast"
**Visual identity:** warm amber, Inter medium wordmark
**Site:** responsive HTML, tabbed demo sections, mobile hamburger nav, forced light mode

## Hero Trailer Video — STATUS: Produced ✅

60-90 second montage, multiple HeyGen public avatars, multiple settings, ~10-15s per scene. Fast cuts intentionally mask AI avatar imperfections. One consistent narrator voice ties the non-avatar scenes together for brand cohesion.

**Final script:**

1. (no avatar, text overlay) — "Every avatar in this video is fully AI generated."
2. (no avatar, narrator + text) — "Your business needs video. Hiring a crew shouldn't be the bottleneck."
3. (Avatar 1, office setting, label: ONBOARDING) — "We make onboarding videos new hires actually remember. No more sitting through a slideshow on day one."
4. (Avatar 2, different setting, label: TRAINING) — "Training content your team can actually follow. Built around how people learn, not just what you need to cover."
5. (workplace B-roll + quick cut to editing timeline, no avatar) — "Every video is produced within 48 to 72 working hours. No production crew, no scheduling headaches."
6. (Avatar 3, course backdrop, label: COURSE CONTENT) — "Course modules, tutorials, certifications, and more, we don't stop at one use case. All built to keep people engaged, the kind they actually finish instead of abandoning halfway through."
7. (logo reveal) — "AI video solutions for businesses that move fast. If you're ready to ship video without the overhead, let's talk."

**Production notes:**
- Avatars chosen for visual diversity (gender, setting, energy) to signal range across AU/UK/Singapore markets
- AI-reveal placed at the front this round. Worth testing an end-reveal variant later (let avatars play first, reveal "everything you just watched was AI" right before the logo), tends to land harder since viewers judge it as normal content first

## Demo Video: Onboarding — STATUS: Script ready, not yet produced

Single avatar, talking head, ~75 seconds. Built to be a real, usable example (not a pitch reel like the trailer), this is "here's exactly what you'd get."

> "Hey, welcome to the team. I know your first day can feel like a lot, so I made this video to walk you through the basics before you dive in.
>
> [cut to screen recording/B-roll: dashboard/tools] First, you'll get access to three things, your email, your project tool, and your team chat. IT will set these up before you arrive, so check your inbox for login details.
>
> [cut back to avatar] Second, your first week is light on purpose. We want you meeting people and learning the workflow, not buried in tasks. Your manager will set up a few intro calls, just show up and ask questions, that's the job this week.
>
> [cut to B-roll: team/office shots] And third, if you ever get stuck, our team channel is the fastest way to get an answer. No question is too small, we'd rather you ask than guess.
>
> [cut back to avatar] That's it. Welcome aboard, we're glad you're here."

**Production notes:** calm/lower energy avatar voice, cut to B-roll every 2-3 sentences, medium shots only (no extreme close ups), captions on, light background music.
